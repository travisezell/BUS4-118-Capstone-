# Ticket FAQs

## Ticket states

IT tickets move through these states: `new` → `in_progress` → `waiting_on_user` or `waiting_on_approval` → `resolved` → `closed`. A ticket in `waiting_on_user` is paused until the requester replies; in `waiting_on_approval` it is paused until a manager or Security reviewer signs off.

## Stale tickets

A ticket without activity for more than 5 business days is considered stale. Stale tickets are auto-escalated to the team lead. If your ticket is stale and still open, reply on the ticket or open a follow-up so it gets re-prioritized.

## SLAs

Standard tickets resolve within 1-3 business days. P1 incidents (outage, data loss, suspected compromise) target 30 minutes for first response and 4 hours for resolution. Access requests are typically 1 business day after manager approval.

## Where to find ticket IDs

Ticket IDs are returned in your confirmation email and visible at the top of every reply from IT. They look like `INC-1042` for incidents or `REQ-2001` for requests. Provide the ID when asking the assistant for status — without it, the assistant has to ask you for more details.
