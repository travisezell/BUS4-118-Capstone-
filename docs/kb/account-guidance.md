# Account Guidance

## Lockout recovery

Most account lockouts after too many failed login attempts auto-clear in 15 minutes. If you are still locked out, use the "Forgot password" link on the SSO sign-in page to send a reset link to your work email.

## Password reset (self-service)

You can reset your password yourself: go to sso.company.com, click "Forgot password," check your work email for the reset link, and choose a new password that meets the requirements (12+ characters, mixed case, a number, a symbol).

## MFA reset

If you lost your MFA device, IT must reset MFA for you — this cannot be done self-service. Open an account support ticket and bring a government-issued photo ID to the IT desk for in-person verification.

## Suspected compromise

If you believe your account was compromised — for example, you see logins from unfamiliar locations or someone changed your settings — Security treats this as a P1 incident. Do not try to fix it yourself. Open a high-priority account ticket immediately and a Security analyst will respond within 30 minutes.

## SSO failures

If SSO returns a generic error after a correct password, try clearing your browser cookies for sso.company.com and signing in again. If the error persists, capture a screenshot of the error code and open an account ticket so IT can check the IdP logs.
